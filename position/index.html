Overview · Executive summary

From PDFs to public maps.

GlassCase is a civic-tech lab that maps how Australian law works in practice. Not how it reads in a statute — how it actually operates when someone lodges a request, challenges a decision, or waits for a response that never comes.

We build open, interactive tools that make the hidden architecture of administrative decisions visible. Where does discretion sit? Where do delays cluster? Where are redactions applied, and why?

The lab produces diagnostic frameworks, data visualisations, and research outputs — all freely available. Our tools are built from real legislative sources and tested against Australian case law.

LightKey, our public accessibility layer, converts this diagnostic work into step-by-step pathway guides for people who deal with government processes — designed with particular attention to people with disability and neurodivergent users.

Why we exist · The problem

The architecture of government decisions is invisible to the people those decisions affect.

Government decisions affect millions of Australians every year. Freedom of Information requests. Migration decisions. Licensing refusals. Disciplinary proceedings. Administrative reviews.

The law that governs these decisions exists. It is written down. In many cases, it works.

But the architecture of these decisions — how they are structured, where discretion lives, where mandatory considerations are applied or missed — is invisible to almost everyone outside the system.

Rules live in Acts, policies, ministerial directions, and agency manuals. There is no shared map. No shared language. No way for a researcher, journalist, or affected person to see the full picture.

The result: decisions that fail review not because the decision-maker got the law wrong, but because a required consideration never appeared in the reasons. Delays that breach statutory timelines with no practical consequence. Redaction patterns that look chaotic from the outside but follow hidden internal logic.

This is not a problem of bad people. It is a problem of invisible architecture.

Identity · What GlassCase is

A diagnostic layer for Australian civic-legal systems.

GlassCase is

A civic-tech lab that maps real administrative and legal pathways. An open-access research platform — tools are free to use. A diagnostic layer that reveals how decisions are structured, not whether specific decisions were right or wrong.

GlassCase is not

A law firm or legal advice service. An advocacy organisation that campaigns for particular outcomes. A replacement for professional legal representation. An AI product — tools are built from legislation and case law, not generated by machine learning.

Founded and led by Jay Spudvilas — an education leader with twelve years of public governance experience and an administrative law researcher at ANU.

What we believe · Principles

Six commitments that shape everything we build.

1
Make what is hidden visible.

If a process affects people, they should be able to see how it works. That includes the rules, the timelines, the discretion points, and the review options.

2
Work from real data.

Every tool is built from legislation, case law, or publicly available administrative data. No tool is released without a traceable evidence base.

3
Open by default.

Tools and frameworks are freely available. Research outputs are published with DOIs through Zenodo (CERN/DataCite). Policy submissions are lodged on the public record.

4
Map the system, not the person.

GlassCase maps how decisions are structured. It does not profile individuals, track personal data, or make predictions about specific outcomes.

5
Design for the people affected.

If a tool only makes sense to lawyers, it has not finished its job. Public-facing translations are delivered through LightKey — GlassCase's accessibility layer.

6
Name what you don't know.

Where evidence is limited or claims are uncertain, we say so. Placeholders are better than false confidence. We use version numbers because tools improve.

Maps institutional stress signals over time using observable data. Score subsystems, detect patterns, identify where deeper inquiry may be needed.

How we work · Method

Start with a question. Trace the pathway. Build the tool.

GlassCase starts with a question: where does this process break down, and why can't people see it?

We identify the relevant legislation. We trace the decision pathway from statute through to agency practice. We look at where discretion enters, where delays cluster, and where the review chain begins.

We build tools from what we find. Each tool version is published with a version number and a DOI. Claims are tested against Australian case law — primarily High Court and Federal Court authorities.

We do not generate tools from AI models. We do not rely on secondary summaries. Every framework traces back to a primary source.

When a tool is ready for a wider audience, the public translation moves to LightKey.

Boundaries · Safety, ethics, and limits

What GlassCase will not do.

GlassCase publishes information, not legal advice. Tools are diagnostic — they show how decisions are structured, not what you should do about a specific decision.

We do not collect personal data beyond basic analytics (with consent). We do not profile users. We do not sell data. Our tools are built from publicly available sources.

Where evidence is uncertain or limited, we say so. We use version numbers because tools improve. v0.1 means the framework is functional but early.

  • We will not provide legal advice or tell you what decision to make
  • We will not access or store personal case information
  • We will not make predictions about the outcome of specific decisions
  • We will not claim authority beyond our evidence base
